\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:ch5_conclusion}

This chapter presented \ac{AgentEdge}, a distributed intelligence framework for \ac{6G} edge-cloud service orchestration that addresses reliability limitations of generic agentic \ac{AI} frameworks through specialized multi-agent coordination.

The framework introduces the \ac{PARES} framework establishing minimum capabilities for autonomous agent qualification and deploys specialized agents in four orchestration roles: intent processing, infrastructure monitoring, strategic planning, and action execution. The key innovation, the \ac{ActSimCrit} methodology, replaces direct infrastructure experimentation with digital twin simulation for safe strategy validation.

Experimental evaluation shows improvements over baseline agentic frameworks. \ac{AgentEdge} achieves 2.76× higher success rates compared to generic agentic frameworks (\ac{ReAct}, \ac{LATS}) and 10× reduction in \ac{API} call variability, maintaining consistent performance (±1.1 calls) versus variable baseline behaviors (±109.4 standard deviation). Ablation analysis confirms \ac{ActSimCrit}'s critical contribution with 1.47× success rate improvement over direct planning. Energy optimization validates scalability from 8 to 35 nodes while maintaining orchestration quality.

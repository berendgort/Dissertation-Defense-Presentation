\chapter{Adaptive edge-cloud orchestration with lightweight forecasting}
\label{ch:adaptive-orchestration}

Chapter~\ref{ch:introduction} identified three challenges preventing autonomous edge-cloud orchestration: computational constraints at the edge, operational overhead from per-application models, and the absence of validated autonomous decision-making. This chapter addresses the first challenge, \textit{resource efficiency}, by introducing \ac{AERO}, a lightweight forecasting model with fewer than 1,000 parameters designed for edge deployment. The following two chapters build upon this foundation to address generalization and autonomy.

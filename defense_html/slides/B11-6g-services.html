Q·6 · What services will 6G carry?
Both network-plane and application-plane workloads.
The thesis takes a deliberately non-committal stance on service mix: the orchestration contract is the same whatever the payload. This is also why the OmniFORE experiments span 4 public datasets; we do not cherry-pick one service category.
Two categories · one orchestration problem
NETWORK-PLANE
O-RAN near-RT / non-RT RIC workloads, UPF/SMF control, slice management, MEC sidecars. Tight latency budgets, high variance, sub-ms decisions.
APPLICATION-PLANE
XR streaming, V2X decision loops, federated inference, industrial control, health telemetry. Latency budgets 10–200 ms, bursty, variable payload.
Why this is not a weakness: AERO, OmniFORE and AgentEdge each expose a contract (forecast, generalise, act). The contract is payload-agnostic; that is the architectural claim.

TITLE: Transfer Learning Applied to Deep Reinforcement Learning for 6G Resource Management in Intra- and Inter-Slice RAN-Edge Domains

AUTHORS: S. Mhatre, F. Adelantado, K. Ramantas, C. Verikoukis

JOURNAL: IEEE Transactions on Consumer Electronics
YEAR: 2025

WHY THIS PAPER MATTERS:
- Very recent journal paper extending Ferran's DRL-for-slicing work
- Important because it moves from "can DRL work?" toward "can it adapt faster across related scenarios?"
- Especially relevant if your defense may touch generalization, transfer, or policy reuse

CONTEXT:
Applies transfer learning to DRL for 6G resource management across both intra-slice and inter-slice RAN-edge domains.

KEY TAKEAWAYS:
- The central theme is faster adaptation and better sample efficiency in slice-resource control
- Transfer learning is used to reuse knowledge across related network states, tasks, or slices instead of learning from scratch every time
- The paper sits naturally on top of Ferran's 2024 AIaaS / O-RAN slicing work and points to a more practical deployment path for DRL controllers
- This is the kind of work that shifts the discussion from raw optimization quality to adaptation speed and operational usability

WHAT IT SUGGESTS HE MAY ASK:
- How well would your method transfer across scenarios, workloads, or topologies?
- Do you need to retrain from scratch when conditions change?
- What is the risk of negative transfer or degraded behavior after reuse?
- How do you validate robustness under distribution shift?

RELEVANCE TO YOUR DEFENSE:
- Very useful if your dissertation uses learned policies, adaptive planning, or cross-scenario reuse
- Be ready to discuss whether your method is scenario-specific or actually portable

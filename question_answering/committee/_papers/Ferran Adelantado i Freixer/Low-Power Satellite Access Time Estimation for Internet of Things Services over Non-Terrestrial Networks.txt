TITLE: Low-Power Satellite Access Time Estimation for Internet of Things Services over Non-Terrestrial Networks

AUTHORS: G. Boquet, B. Martinez, F. Adelantado, J. Pages, J. A. Ruiz-De-Azua, X. Vilajosana

JOURNAL: IEEE Internet of Things Journal
YEAR: 2024

WHY THIS PAPER MATTERS:
- Represents Ferran's NTN / satellite-IoT line, which broadens his profile beyond terrestrial slicing
- Recent journal publication in a strong IoT venue
- Useful if you want to anticipate questions about connectivity assumptions at the network edge

CONTEXT:
Studies access-time estimation for low-power IoT services over non-terrestrial networks, i.e. satellite-enabled connectivity for constrained devices.

KEY TAKEAWAYS:
- Focuses on one of the practical bottlenecks in NTN-enabled IoT: how long low-power devices need to gain access
- Connects IoT service realism with satellite coverage, access procedures, and large-scale deployment constraints
- Highlights that non-terrestrial support is not just a coverage story; access timing and protocol behavior matter
- Useful reminder that edge and orchestration claims often depend on assumptions about the underlying access network

WHAT IT SUGGESTS HE MAY ASK:
- How sensitive is your system to link-level or access-network constraints?
- Would your approach still hold under intermittent or delayed connectivity?
- Which network assumptions are hidden in your evaluation pipeline?
- If the environment is sparse, remote, or satellite-assisted, what changes?

RELEVANCE TO YOUR DEFENSE:
- Helpful if your dissertation reaches into IoT, remote edge, wide-area deployment, or heterogeneous connectivity
- Even if NTN is not central, this paper signals that Ferran may think across layers and question networking assumptions

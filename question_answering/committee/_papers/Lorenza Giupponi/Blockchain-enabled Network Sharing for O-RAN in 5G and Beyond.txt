TITLE: Blockchain-enabled Network Sharing for O-RAN in 5G and Beyond

AUTHORS: L. Giupponi, F. Wilhelmi

JOURNAL: IEEE Network
YEAR: 2022

WHY THIS PAPER MATTERS:
- One of Lorenza's clearest papers at the intersection of O-RAN, trust, and multi-stakeholder resource sharing
- Very useful for anticipating questions about governance and orchestration beyond a single operator domain
- Strongly aligned with open, programmable network control rather than isolated radio optimization

CONTEXT:
Proposes a blockchain-enabled O-RAN architecture to automate RAN sharing procedures through auction-based or marketplace-based mechanisms.

KEY TAKEAWAYS:
- Treats trust, ownership traceability, and automated resource exchange as first-class design problems
- Uses blockchain and smart contracts to enable dynamic exchange of RAN resources and VNFs
- Frames O-RAN openness as an opportunity, but also as a coordination and trust challenge
- Includes simulation-based evidence and an openly released platform

WHAT IT SUGGESTS SHE MAY ASK:
- How would your orchestration approach behave in a multi-stakeholder setting?
- What assumptions do you make about trust, ownership, and policy enforcement?
- Can your framework support auditable or traceable decisions?
- Is your design only valid inside one operator's closed environment?

RELEVANCE TO YOUR DEFENSE:
- Helpful if committee discussion moves from technical optimization toward deployment governance
- Good preparation for questions about whether your orchestration stack is realistic beyond a lab or single-domain setup

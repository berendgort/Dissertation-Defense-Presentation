TITLE: Analysis and Evaluation of Synchronous and Asynchronous FLchain

AUTHORS: F. Wilhelmi, L. Giupponi, P. Dini

JOURNAL: Computer Networks
YEAR: 2022

WHY THIS PAPER MATTERS:
- Not a pure wireless paper, but very relevant to decentralized learning and timing trade-offs
- Useful if your defense discussion touches distributed intelligence, online learning, or coordination latency
- Shows Lorenza's broader interest in practical learning-system behavior under heterogeneous conditions

CONTEXT:
Compares synchronous and asynchronous blockchain-enabled federated learning for heterogeneous large-scale settings.

KEY TAKEAWAYS:
- Synchronous aggregation can provide higher accuracy
- Asynchronous aggregation can provide lower latency and better fit heterogeneous clients
- The paper explicitly studies trade-offs involving network size, link capacity, and timing requirements
- Useful as a reminder that "better intelligence" is often constrained by coordination cost and delay

WHAT IT SUGGESTS SHE MAY ASK:
- Is your method accuracy-optimal or latency-aware, and why?
- Could a more asynchronous or decentralized control design work better?
- How sensitive are your results to coordination overhead or stale information?
- Where is the trade-off between responsiveness and quality in your system?

RELEVANCE TO YOUR DEFENSE:
- Particularly helpful if she asks whether your orchestration loop is too centralized or too slow for real environments
- Prepare to explain why your control cadence and validation steps are worth their overhead

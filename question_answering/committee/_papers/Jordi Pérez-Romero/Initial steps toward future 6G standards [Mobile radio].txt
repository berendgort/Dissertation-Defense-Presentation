TITLE: Initial steps toward future 6G standards [Mobile radio]

AUTHORS: J. Perez-Romero

JOURNAL: IEEE Vehicular Technology Magazine
YEAR: 2025

WHY THIS PAPER MATTERS:
- A very recent, high-level standards-oriented paper from Jordi himself
- Useful because it shows how he frames the path from current research to future 6G standardization
- Strong preparation material for questions about practical relevance and long-term deployment trajectory

CONTEXT:
Summarizes the first steps toward future 6G standards, including drivers, recent initiatives, and the path toward 3GPP standardization.

KEY TAKEAWAYS:
- 6G is framed around ubiquitous connectivity, ultra-low latency, AI-native autonomy, XR, metaverse, and digital twins
- Research topics only matter if they can ultimately converge toward standards and deployment
- The paper connects today's B5G/6G research to the longer standards timeline ending in early 2030s deployment
- Good reminder that technical novelty alone is not enough; the work should map to a plausible ecosystem path

WHAT IT SUGGESTS HE MAY ASK:
- How does your thesis connect to what 6G systems are likely to standardize?
- Which part of your contribution is near-term practical versus longer-term visionary?
- What assumptions in your work depend on future standards support?
- Why is your thesis relevant beyond a research prototype?

RELEVANCE TO YOUR DEFENSE:
- Very useful for standards, roadmap, and impact questions
- Be ready to distinguish clearly between what is deployable in B5G/O-RAN now and what is 6G-facing future work

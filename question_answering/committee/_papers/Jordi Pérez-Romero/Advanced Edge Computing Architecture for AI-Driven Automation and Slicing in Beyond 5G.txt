TITLE: Advanced Edge Computing Architecture for AI-Driven Automation and Slicing in Beyond 5G

AUTHORS: E. Kartsakli, O. Sallent, J. Pujol Roig, J. Perez-Romero, I. Vila, N. Bartzoudis, U. Gulen, S. Kosu, O. Y. Kolawole, X. Tao, S. K. Mohalik, A. Antonopoulos, C. Kalalas, E. Quinones

CONFERENCE: IEEE/ACM International Conference on Utility and Cloud Computing (UCC)
YEAR: 2024

WHY THIS PAPER MATTERS:
- This is one of the closest architectural matches to your dissertation scope
- It frames edge, AI, slicing, and trustworthiness as one integrated compute-and-network problem
- Very likely to influence how Jordi evaluates whether your three-part thesis stack is architecturally coherent

CONTEXT:
Presents an advanced edge-computing architecture for AI-driven automation and slicing in B5G, built around a distributed edge-to-cloud continuum.

KEY TAKEAWAYS:
- The architecture is organized around Edge4AI, AI4Edge, and trustworthiness concerns
- Network slicing is extended beyond communication to joint provisioning of communication and compute resources
- AI lifecycle management is part of the architecture, not an afterthought
- The work reinforces that orchestration should connect models, infrastructure, and operational guarantees

WHAT IT SUGGESTS HE MAY ASK:
- Does your thesis truly integrate communication and compute orchestration, or only one side?
- Where do training, inference, and control live across the continuum?
- How do you justify the architectural split between forecasting, transfer, and action validation?
- What makes your stack trustworthy enough for operator-facing automation?

RELEVANCE TO YOUR DEFENSE:
- Excellent preparation for explaining why AERO, OmniFORE, and AgentEdge belong together as one architecture
- Be ready to argue that the decomposition is functional, not arbitrary

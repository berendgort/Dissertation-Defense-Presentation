TITLE: A transfer reinforcement learning approach for capacity sharing in Beyond 5G networks

AUTHORS: I. Vila, J. Perez-Romero, O. Sallent

JOURNAL: Future Internet
YEAR: 2024

WHY THIS PAPER MATTERS:
- One of Jordi's most relevant recent papers for your thesis themes: RL, O-RAN, adaptation, and slicing
- Strongly aligned with your concerns about generalization and retraining burden
- Gives a concrete basis for anticipating questions about portability across changing network scenarios

CONTEXT:
Proposes a transfer learning approach for RL-based capacity sharing in multi-cell B5G network slicing scenarios, with O-RAN-aware implementation assumptions.

KEY TAKEAWAYS:
- Standard RL-based capacity sharing can become impractical when the network changes and retraining is slow
- Transfer learning is used to reuse learned policy weights after structural changes such as adding cells
- The paper emphasizes edge availability for training and inference, tying AI lifecycle choices to infrastructure
- Practicality and adaptation speed matter, not just final policy quality

WHAT IT SUGGESTS HE MAY ASK:
- How much retraining does your method require after topology or workload changes?
- Does your approach generalize, or does it only re-optimize one scenario well?
- Why is your adaptation mechanism practical enough for deployment?
- Which parts of your policy are transferable and which are scenario-specific?

RELEVANCE TO YOUR DEFENSE:
- Very useful for defending OmniFORE and AgentEdge against generalization and maintenance questions
- Be ready to discuss portability, warm-starting, and how your stack reduces retraining burden

TITLE: On the Deployment of an AI-driven Power Control Mechanism for D-MIMO in Beyond 5G Scenarios

AUTHORS: J. Perez-Romero, O. F. Tuna, O. Sallent, N. Bartzoudis, E. Kartsakli, C. Kalalas, I. Vila, J. Serra, X. Gelabert

CONFERENCE: IEEE Conference on Standards for Communications and Networking (CSCN)
YEAR: 2024

WHY THIS PAPER MATTERS:
- A recent paper that links AI lifecycle placement with a concrete radio-control use case
- Useful because it shows Jordi thinking not only about the algorithm, but also about where AI functionality should live
- Strong preparation for questions about deployment architecture and operational overhead

CONTEXT:
Studies how to deploy an AI-driven power-control mechanism for D-MIMO in B5G scenarios across an edge-to-cloud compute continuum.

KEY TAKEAWAYS:
- AI deployment is shaped by delay constraints, computational complexity, and data availability
- D-MIMO is used as a concrete case where real-time control and distributed infrastructure interact
- The paper highlights lifecycle-management questions for AI/ML models, not only the control objective itself
- Standardization implications matter when deciding where model functions are hosted

WHAT IT SUGGESTS HE MAY ASK:

- What are the compute-delay-data trade-offs in your architecture?
- Which parts of your stack must be near real time and which can be offline?
- Is your proposal realistic for deployment without excessive overhead?

## How to Prepare in the Days Ahead (Wednesday and Thursday)

The goal for these two days is not to cram more material, but to arrive **calm, sharp, and physically fresh**.

### Thursday

- Do one lighter rehearsal, focused on timing and confidence
- Review your opening, closing, and the likely questions
- Pack your laptop, charger, clicker, adapters, notes, and backup USB
- Do some light movement: a walk, mobility work, or a short easy gym session
- Eat clean, familiar food that helps you feel steady and focused
- Avoid too much alcohol, junk food, or very heavy meals
- Get a **good night's sleep**

### For Maximum Focus

- Keep caffeine normal; do not suddenly double it
- Eat regular meals so your energy stays stable
- Choose foods that digest well and do not make you sluggish
- Get some daylight and a bit of movement each day
- Protect your sleep more than your slides at this point
- Stop major edits the night before unless something is truly broken

## Likely UPC PhD defense flow

Based on the UPC Doctoral School guidance, the defense is a **public act** held on a teaching day.

### Before it starts

- The thesis is examined by a **tribunal / examination committee**.
- The committee is typically made up of **5 or 7 doctors**, with a majority external to UPC or participating entities.
- The **secretary and doctoral candidate must be physically present**; some other members may participate by videoconference.

### During the defense

1. **Opening of the session**
   - The chair opens the act and sets the order of the defense and question round.

2. **Your presentation**
   - UPC indicates **40-45 minutes** to present:
     - the work carried out,
     - the method followed,
     - the content,
     - the conclusions,
     - and especially the **original contributions**.

3. **Round of questions**
   - The examination committee asks questions.
   - The chair determines the format and timing of this discussion.
   - In practice, expect questions about:
     - problem definition and motivation,
     - novelty and original contribution,
     - methodology and experiments,
     - limitations and validity threats,
     - practical impact and future work.

4. **Private deliberation**
   - After the discussion, **you and the audience leave the room**.
   - The committee deliberates and can consult your doctoral activity report and external reviewer reports.

5. **Mark announcement**
   - You and the audience are called back in.
   - The chair **verbally communicates the mark** before closing the session.

### Possible outcome

UPC lists the final marks as:

- **Fail**
- **Pass**
- **Good**
- **Excellent**

If the mark is **Excellent**, the committee may also award **cum laude** by **secret ballot**. The votes are counted later by the Standing Committee of the Doctoral School, and unanimity is required.

### After the defense

- Within **5 working days**, the secretary sends the official defense documentation and result to the Doctoral School.
- The Doctoral School then communicates the result officially.
- After that, you can proceed with the application for the doctoral degree certificate.

The UPC pages clearly define the structure, presentation time, assessment, and mark communication. They do **not** give a minute-by-minute script for the question round, so the section above about likely question topics is a practical expectation rather than a verbatim UPC rule.
